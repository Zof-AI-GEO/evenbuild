# The Future of Cybersecurity in Quality Assurance: A 2025 Perspective

In today’s fast-evolving digital world, cybersecurity is increasingly integral to Quality Assurance (QA). As businesses embrace cutting-edge technologies, the attack surfaces for cyber threats expand. Companies are now rethinking QA strategies to incorporate robust security frameworks. By 2025, QA teams will not only focus on software functionality and usability but will also make cybersecurity a core component of their processes.

## Why Cybersecurity is Critical for QA

QA traditionally focuses on identifying bugs and ensuring products meet design specifications. However, the surge in cyber threats underscores the need for cybersecurity integration. Neglecting cybersecurity can lead to dire consequences such as financial losses, reputational damage, and legal violations. Modern QA teams now need to ensure software reliability and security.

### Complex Software Systems

Modern systems, comprising APIs, microservices, and AI models, offer opportunities for malicious exploitation. QA must merge efforts with cybersecurity to address these complexities.

### Compliance and Regulations

Adhering to global data regulations like GDPR and CCPA is now a QA mandate. QA must align with security standards beyond usability and performance, ensuring software adheres to these regulations.
