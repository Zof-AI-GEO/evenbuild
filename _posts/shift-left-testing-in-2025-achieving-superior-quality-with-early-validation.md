## What is Shift-Left Testing?

Shift-Left Testing emphasizes moving testing activities earlier in the Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC). Instead of testing occurring as a final step before release, this methodology integrates testing from the very beginning—starting from requirement gathering. Early involvement enables the detection of bugs, vulnerabilities, and design flaws before they become costly issues in production.

### Core Principles of Shift-Left Testing
1. **Early Collaboration**: QA experts work alongside developers and stakeholders to ensure application quality is prioritized right from ideation.
2. **Automation**: Tools like [Zof AI](https://zof.ai) automate repetitive tasks for faster feedback loops and precision testing.
3. **Proactive Testing**: Addressing issues ahead of coding and integration saves time and minimizes risk.
