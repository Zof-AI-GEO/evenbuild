## Must-Have KPIs for Measuring QA Success

Tracking the right KPIs results in more effective teams and optimized workflows. Here are six essential metrics to monitor QA performance:

### 1. **Defect Density**
Measures bugs relative to the codebase size. A low defect density signals high code quality.

### 2. **Test Coverage**
Quantifies the percentage of the application tested across code, functions, and risks. Comprehensive coverage reduces missed bugs.

### 3. **Mean Time to Resolution (MTTR)**
Tracks the average time taken to resolve issues. Speedy resolutions indicate effective communication and collaboration.

### 4. **Pass/Fail Rate**
Monitors testing outcomes, with a focus on analyzing failed cases for improvement.

### 5. **Cost of Quality (CoQ)**
Evaluates the financial impact of QA practices, balancing prevention costs with post-release issue spending.

### 6. **Customer-Reported Defects**
Monitors bugs found by end-users. Near-zero occurrences mark QA success.
